Self-CTRL

Self-Consistency Training with Reinforcement Learning · MIT CSAIL · Jacob Andreas Lab · paper ↗

Overview

Language models often explain their behavior in ways that don't match what they actually do. This project develops a policy-gradient method that trains models to be consistent between their self-explanations and their behavior, so that a model's stated reasoning is a faithful account of how it actually acts.

The approach improves joint consistency from 43% to 96%, and as a safety benefit, reduces the HarmBench failure rate from 15.0% to 0.5%. The work is written up in Self-CTRL: Self-Consistency Training with Reinforcement Learning.

Highlights

  • Policy-gradient training that aligns model explanations with model behavior.
  • Joint consistency improved from 43% to 96%.
  • HarmBench failure rate reduced from 15.0% to 0.5%.
